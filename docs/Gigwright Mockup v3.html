Gig sheet · email

The one-tap email that goes out when you hit "Send gig alert." Full gig sheet — times, address, attire, personnel, notes — minus pay. Pay stays a private conversation between you and each musician.
Email · part of "send gig alert"

One tap sends this to the whole band.

When you hit "Send gig alert" in the app, every musician on the gig gets a clean, email-client-safe version of the gig sheet — times, address as tap-to-Maps, attire, personnel, notes, set list link. No pay amounts. Pay stays between you and each musician.

  • i.Pay-free. Money never goes through this channel. The musician knows their rate from the phone call you made before booking them.
  • ii.Email-client safe. Inline CSS, table-based layout, system font fallbacks. Renders in Gmail, Apple Mail, Outlook, iPhone Mail.
  • iii.Printable. Same template exports clean as PDF if someone wants a paper copy.
  • iv.Diff-aware. If you've updated the gig since last send, the email flags what changed at the top.

The band · SMS

No app. No login.
Just the details.

The text carries the same information as the email — full gig sheet, in their hand, on the device they actually check. Times, address, attire, who else is playing, notes, and a link to the set list and add-on charts. No pay amounts — that stays a private conversation between you and each musician.

  • i.Booked. One full-detail text with everything from the gig sheet, plus a tap-to-Maps address and a link to the set list and charts.
  • ii.Changed. If anything moves — time, attire, set list, charts — only the diff goes out, not the whole message again. "Downbeat now 8:00 (was 7:30)" stands on its own.
  • iii.Attachment changed. If the set list is swapped or the gig-materials link is updated, the band gets an auto-notification.
  • iv.Morning of. Quiet reminder with the address and call time.
  • v.Ask to confirm, optional. Flip on per-gig. Default is info-only — you already called them.
